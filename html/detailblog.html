Tips & suggestions: Travel around Bali?

Feb 17, 2024
2 comment

Before traveling, carefully plan your destinations to make the most of your Bali experience. The island offers diverse attractions for every type of traveler, including serene beaches like Kuta, Seminyak, Nusa Dua, and Jimbaran, historic temples such as Uluwatu, Tanah Lot, Besakih, and Tirta Empul, and stunning mountainous regions like Ubud, Kintamani, and Munduk. For those seeking adventure, activities like snorkeling in Amed, surfing in Canggu, or trekking Mount Batur provide unforgettable experiences.

For transportation, Bali provides various convenient options. Renting a motorbike is an affordable and flexible choice for solo travelers or couples, with prices ranging from Rp50,000 to Rp100,000 per day. For families or groups, renting a car with a driver costs around Rp400,000 to Rp800,000 per day. Apps like Gojek or Grab are perfect for short trips. Plan your visit during the dry season (April to October) for pleasant weather, but steer clear of peak holiday seasons like December, Eid, and August if you seek a quieter atmosphere.

While in Bali, respecting local customs is essential. Dress modestly when visiting temples, where sarongs and sashes are often required and can usually be borrowed on-site. Avoid stepping on offerings (canang) placed on the ground as they hold spiritual significance. Traffic congestion in popular areas such as Kuta, Seminyak, and Ubud can be challenging, so always adhere to traffic rules and wear a helmet when riding a motorbike. For a peaceful escape, visit hidden gems like Nyang Nyang Beach, Pinggan Village, or Sekumpul Waterfall. End your day with a breathtaking sunset at iconic spots like Uluwatu, Jimbaran, or Tanah Lot.

Bali also offers a wide range of activities for relaxation and adventure alike. Try watersports at Tanjung Benoa, including parasailing, banana boat rides, and jet skiing. Explore underwater beauty through snorkeling or diving at Nusa Penida, Tulamben, or Menjangan Island. For tranquility, Ubud provides world-class yoga retreats and soothing spa experiences. To ensure comfort, keep your belongings secure, use sunscreen, and drink bottled water. Staying connected is easy with a local SIM card from providers like Telkomsel or XL.

No Bali trip is complete without savoring its culinary delights. Don’t miss local dishes such as Ayam Betutu, Babi Guling, Sate Lilit, and Lawar, which offer rich flavors unique to Balinese culture. Bring a piece of Bali home by shopping for handicrafts at Sukawati Art Market or indulging in local treats like Pie Susu and Kacang Bali. Lastly, immerse yourself in Bali’s rich cultural heritage by observing and respecting traditional ceremonies and customs, which provide a deeper connection to the island’s unique charm.
